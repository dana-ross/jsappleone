By now you've no doubt heard the story of the two Steves and their radical idea of a computer so inexpensive that regular people could afford it. The Apple I computerwas born in a California garage. combining Steve Jobs' drive and business acument with Steve Wozniak's unconventional technological wizardry. It was meant to be sold as just a circuit board populated with some chips, but The Byte Shop had their own wooden cases made. Users crafted their own cases as well.

The Apple I was driven by a MOS 6502 processor running at about 1MHz. It came with 4 kilobytes of RAM and a "monitor" program in ROM.
